# AI Summary Quality Control Checklist

Applied to neighborhood content generated by the Neighborhood Content Generator workflow.
Use this checklist for manual spot-checks and as the basis for automated validation.

## Uniqueness Checks

- [ ] Does the summary contain at least 3 data points unique to this specific neighborhood's ZIP code or location? (median home value, specific school name, specific POI)
- [ ] Does the summary mention at least one named local landmark, park, or road that is geographically accurate?
- [ ] Would this summary be factually wrong if you swapped in a different neighborhood name? If the answer is no — it's too generic

## Accuracy Checks

- [ ] Do all dollar figures trace back to a specific data source? (ACS, TREC)
- [ ] Are school names verified against TEA data for that ZIP/district?
- [ ] Is the neighborhood type (POA vs COA) correctly reflected in the language?
- [ ] Does the city/county match the TREC certificate — not just the ZIP centroid?

## Prose Quality Checks

- [ ] Is the summary at least 80 words of flowing prose — not bullet points in disguise?
- [ ] Does it avoid phrases that appear verbatim on other pages? (run a duplicate string check on sentences over 12 words)
- [ ] Does it open with something other than "X is a property owners association located in..."? (flag if more than 20% of summaries share an opening pattern)
- [ ] Does it avoid stating the obvious? ("A neighborhood manages common areas" adds nothing)

## FAQ Checks

- [ ] Does each FAQ answer contain at least one data point specific to this neighborhood or its ZIP?
- [ ] Are the answers different enough from neighboring neighborhood pages in the same ZIP? (If two neighborhoods share a ZIP, their county fee question answers will be identical — that's acceptable, flag it as known duplication)
- [ ] Does the "What is X neighborhood?" answer differ meaningfully from the about_body — not just a shorter version of it?

## Red Flag Patterns to Auto-Detect

These are strings your pipeline should flag for human review before publishing:

- Summary under 60 words
- No named school in the summary
- No named road, landmark, or POI in the summary
- Median home value missing or showing a state/national average instead of ZIP-level
- Any sentence appearing on more than 5% of all neighborhood pages verbatim
- FAQ answers under 25 words

## Human Review Threshold

Not every page needs human eyes — but flag for manual review if:

- The neighborhood has no matching ZIP demographics (data gap)
- The neighborhood city doesn't match any known neighborhood in your system
- The TREC certificate is expired or flagged
- The summary score fails 3 or more of the above checks

## The Litmus Test

A human reviewer spot-checking 20 random pages can't predict what the next page will say. If they can — the summaries are too templated.
